**The Goals of this System**  
This game is a turn-based, tactical tabletop role-playing game. Its primary focuses are:

 1\. Creating interesting scenarios that engender player investment, both pre-written from within the module and (perhaps even more importantly) by encouraging ludic moments by allowing players to logically form their own solutions to obstacles both narrative and emergent.

2\. Allowing the mechanical character choices (attribute point distribution, split level-up decisions, etc.), the equipment systems, and the “Talents” system to open up myriad opportunities for character builds, i.e. each player feeling like they can almost handcraft their character to their liking-- each choice, regardless of the player’s rationale can lead to the creation of a useful party member while rewarding those who go a step further, plotting their build several levels in advance or those who solely make their decisions entirely while role-playing their characters.

# CHARACTER CREATION

1: Familiarize yourself with the nine attribute stats- what they do and how they affect your character’s progression  
2: Select your character’s tribe, class and any starting skills / spells they should gain based off of those selections  
3\. Add **\+1** to an Attribute and **\-1** to an Attribute as you see fit (*can cancel each other out if desired; recommended to be done last*)

# CHARACTER ATTRIBUTES

These nine attributes are the values that are added to every roll a player will make in a game. Choosing which attributes to focus or to ignore will drastically change your character's strengths and weaknesses; They are the core element to character building.

**THE MAXIMUM VALUE AN ATTRIBUTE CAN BE RAISED TO IS \+7**

**STRENGTH** *(STR)  The physical power that one can call upon through rigorous training*  
\- Increases melee DMG (damage) and encumbrance / Required for 4 Talents  
**ACCURACY** *(ACC) The acuity of one's sight and body coordinating as one*  
 \- Increases all ATK (attack) rolls and ranged weapon DMG / Required for 6 Talents  
**FINESSE** *(FNS) The deftness of one’s reflexive actions and fine movements*  
 \- Increases EVA (evasion) / Each investment raises Critical Hit rate by 1 / Required for 5 Talents  
**SPEED** *(SPD) The power behind one’s movements both ambulatory and regarding combat actions*  
 \- Increases movement range, blocking, and Martial Maneuver rolls / Required for 2 Talents  
**INTELLIGENCE** *(INT) The sharpness of one’s mind for magical, observational, and academic purposes*  
 \- Increases Spellcast rolls / Required for 3 Talents  
**MEMORY** *(MEM) The capacity of one’s mind for memorization, recollection, and articulation*  
 \- Increases Spell Memory / Required for 4 Talents  
**VITALITY** *(VIT) Endurance and willpower; The strain one’s physical form can withstand*  
 \- Increases Maximum HP & Stamina / Required for 2 Talents  
**CHARISMA** *(CHA) The arts of speechcraft, bartering, and the quality of silver coating one’s tongue*  
 \- Increases all NPC speech-related / Increases Luck / Required for 2 Talents  
**SURVIVAL** *(SRV) The art of honing one’s instincts to razor-sharp precision*  
*\-* Increases no stat values but is all but required for lengthy adventures / Required for 4 Talents

# PLAYABLE TRIBES

There are seven playable tribes/species/races (what have you) each with their own strengths, shortcomings, and unique abilities. 

* **LAMIIRAN \-** *A hardy, longer lived offshoot of humanity. They are known for their keen eyes and natural survival instinct as well as their cultural exclusivity and their ancestors' penchant for warmongering. They are perhaps still the most populous demographic today.*  
* **GOTHRIN \-** *Taller than their Lamiiran cousins, these human descendants were created by the God of Transformation and Chaos and have retained their creators’ magical prowess and love of change. They were (usually unwilling) partners in many of the Lamiiran Empire’s wars during the Age of Conquest.*  
* **MAZMERI \-** *Descendants of the dwarves and gnomes of old, this short statured race retain features of both their lineages while gaining an industrious nature from the former and a natural curiosity from the latter. Mazmeri created many marvels of magical engineering before the collapse of the old world.*  
* **SOLAR ELF \-** *The last known surviving race of elves in this realm; They are tall, muscular and as a society adept in magic, craftsmanship, the arts of war, and self-sufficiency. They can often be seen with a perpetual scowl, even if no ill-will is meant by them.*  
* **DYNE \-** *Once a plague upon the empires of man and elf while their “queen” controlled their bodies and minds, these hulking insectoid people are strong and sturdy making them reliable warriors and scouts if they can find clients that do not hold to ancient hatreds.*  
* **URANURA \-***A race of massive frogpeople forced to flee from their island home after the pollution of the Age of Conquest made it unlivable. All able-bodied Uranura are strong and swift, each with an imposing figure.*  
* **TENANT \-** *Strange creatures that were once living beings, now merely souls bound to ancient armaments. While the memories are hazy, they typically retain knowledge of their past lives, though some choose to ignore this and start anew. They are almost always seen as outsiders to other groups but are often sought out and exploited for their strange abilities anyway.*

# CLASSES

There are five starting classes in the world of Fight & Prosper that determine a new character’s main suite of abilities and equipment available to use from their beginning as an adventurer .

* **FIGHTER \-** *A warrior with the skill to adapt a vast array of weapons and armor*  
* **SCOUT \-** *Tricky and tactical; Trains in the arts of reconnaissance, ambush and survivalism*  
* **MAGE \-** *An adept spellcaster that can focus their magics on combat or support*  
* **PROVISIONER \-** *An item expert and appraiser with skills that aid in mercantilism and crafting*  
* **PILGRIM \-** *Combatants that excel in support, magical defense, and battling abominations*

# ADVANCED CLASSES

Each of the starting classes can be promoted to an advanced class that greatly hones in on their specific strengths while also granting powerful new skills. This ascension can be chosen  starting at Level 6\.

* **FIGHTER → MYRMIDON**  
  *A combat master that focuses on hitting harder and faster with the goal of decimating enemy forces no matter their numbers or their chosen tactics.*  
* **SCOUT → ROGUE**  
  *Tamer of the wilds and master of infiltration and fabian tactics. The class you call upon for missions deep into uncharted and dangerous territories.*  
* **MAGE → WIZARD**  
  *A dangerously experienced spellcaster with unparalleled capabilities whether their chosen forte is destruction, healing, conjuring illusions, or manipulating physical reality itself.*  
* **PROVISIONER → MERCHANT**  
  *The lynchpin of any profitable party in terms of generating funds, useful goods, or protecting what is already in the group’s possession.*  
* **PILGRIM → WAR PRIEST**  
  *One who has heeded the call of divine forces to protect this plane from the heinous forces of monsterkind and the undead.*

# CHARACTER EQUIPMENT

Each player can have the following equipped under standard circumstances (always remember that carry capacity / encumbrance must be accounted for when equipping items):

- 3 Armament Slots worth of weapons and offhand items  
* 1 Armament slot is equivalent to 1 item weight. Within these parameters any equipped piece of gear can be swapped to on your turn without taking an action to do so. Equipment taking up Armament slots is worn on the body openly.  
- 1 Holdout Weapon  
* A weapon that has the properties of an Armament Slot but does not take up an Armament Slot  
* Must be a Knife, Bow, Hazer, or Throwing weapon that is one-handed and has a weight value of 1 (or less)  
- 2 quick-use items equipped to the player’s toolbelt  
* These items only take your Instant Action to use; Only Consumables, Tools and Grenades can be equipped to the toolbelt  
- 1 Main set of Armor / Clothing (Torso Equipment)  
- 1 Magic Ring  
- 1 Artifact (Amulets, instruments, enchanted baubles, etc.)  
- 1 Helmet (Additional Armor)  
- 1 Pair of Gloves (Additional Armor)  
- 1 Pair of Footwear / Leg Armor (Additional Armor)


**Additional Notes:**

- *Players aren’t told the exact function of certain magic items: rings or legendary gear, nor are they told if one such item is cursed*  
- *Players can learn the nature of such items by blindly equipping them or by utilizing the ‘Diviner’ Talent*  
- *Ring effects / curses are learned upon equipping the item; Legendary weapon / armor effects are learned the first time they are used in battle.*

**Broken Equipment:**

- Broken weapons lower their DMG roll by 1 DT  
- Broken Armor, Shield items, and Tools can not be used at all until repaired

Broken equipment can be repaired by paying a smith to do so or by gathering the required material(s) and using an action at camp to make the repair if a party member has the ‘Armorer’ Talent (a Smithing Hammer is required to use the Armorer Talent).

# LEVELING UP

Each time the party completes a goal or accomplishes a task they will receive a Merit Point, a value attributed to the character that denotes a general growth in acumen, power and instincts. Upon gaining enough Merit to reach the next character level the player gains the following bonuses:

1. **\+2 Training Points :** *Points that can be used to increase Attributes by 1 (up to \+7) or to learn a new training (Advanced Trainings cost both Training Points gained in a level)*  
2. **HP+ :** *Gain 1 HP for leveling up plus 1 additional pt. if VIT was increased this level (increasing VIT with both your ATTR pts. still only grants 1 bonus HP max each)*  
3. **Advancement Bonus :** *(at Lvs. 3,6,9,12 & 15 only) Gain 1 of the 10 Advancement Bonuses granted by your Tribe (these can be selected in any order)*  
4. **Current Lv. Class Progression :** *gain the next ability, passive, etc. on your class sheet (or one of your Specialization progression bonuses)*  
   

* **Reach Level 2 at :** *3 Merit*  
* **Reach Level 3 at :** *8 Merit*  
* **Reach Level 4 at :** *15 Merit*  
* **Reach Level 5 at :** *24 Merit*  
* **Reach Level 6 at :** *35 Merit*  
* **Reach Level 7 at :** *48 Merit*  
* **Reach Level 8 at :** *63 Merit*  
* **Reach Level 9 at :** *80 Merit*  
* **Reach Level 10 at :** *99 Merit*  
* **Reach Level 11 at :** *120 Merit*  
* **Reach Level 12 at :** *143 Merit*  
* **Reach Level 13 at :** *168 Merit*  
* **Reach Level 14 at :** *195 Merit*  
* **Reach Level 15 at :** *225 Merit*

Merit is gained from completing the following tasks (bonus Merit may be awarded based on player participation, thinking outside the box, etc. at the DM’s discretion):

- **Winning a Combat Encounter** (1)  
- **Defeating a Boss Enemy** (1 per)  
- **Discovering a new location** *(one not pre-marked on player maps)* (1)  
- **Defeating an Enemy Party with a higher cumulative Level than your own** (1)  
- **Successfully Completing a Side-Quest** (?) *(Quest rewards may vary)*  
- **Successfully Completing a Main Quest step / chapter** (?) *(Quest rewards may vary)*  
- **Creative Improvisation** (?) *(DM’s Discretion)*


**Challenger Bonus:** *A bonus Merit Point is also awarded to Player Characters that survive combat encounters or complete quests while they are below the minimum recommended character level as designated by the current Module in play.*

# TALENTS

Players can gain permanent character skills by reaching certain Attribute values. These skills are gained immediately upon reaching the required Attribute totals and become a contextual part of the character’s available actions.

- **Armorer:** (STR \[2\] ACC \[2\]) *Can craft, repair and modify weapons and armor (complex equipment like Bowshots can’t be repaired or modified with this Talent) in safe areas like villages and at camp if the player has Forge Hammer and the required material(s) Can also temporarily sharpen weapons at camp with the proper tools.*  
- ***Tailor:*** (ACC \[1\] FNS \[2\]) *Can craft, repair and modify clothing items with Arthosium Thread Weaving in safe areas like villages and at camp if the player has Tailor’s Kit and the required material(s)*  
- **Lockpick:** (FNS \[2\]) *Can use a lock picking tool to attempt to crack locked doors, containers, etc.*   
- **Trapper:** (FNS \[3\]) *Can set up traps and use a Trap Probe to disarm pre-set traps for your own use.*  
- **Counterfeiter** (ACC \[4\] MEM \[2\]) *can replicate small items like documents, keys, coins, etc. however, an observant NPC may recognize your item as a fake (certain materials may be required that the DM will specify: e.g. metal for key forging)*  
- **Diviner:** (INT \[3\] CHA \[2\]) *Can magically infer the usage / purpose of magic items without an Arcana check; Grants permanent advantage on other Arcana checks*  
- **Chef:** (SRV \[2\]) *Can use ingredient items to make meals at camp*  
- **Analyst:** (SRV \[2\] MEM \[2\]) *Can learn an enemy unit’s weak point (1D20+INT / 13+ grants this knowledge / can only be done once per unit type per battle).*  
- **Alchemist:** (MEM \[3\]) *Can combine chemistry ingredients at camp using an Alchemist Kit to craft potions, oils, etc. Also allows for the transmutation of some materials into others during rest / camp. Roll to create an item: 1D20+MEM / 1-5 \= crafting failed / 6-15 \= crafting successful / 16-19 \= item gains \+1 recovery, DMG, potency, etc. / 20 \= item gains \+2 to its values*  
- **Medic:** (VIT \[2\] SRV \[2\]) *Can determine the nature of injuries, cause of death, identify diseases, and parse the approximate HP remaining of enemies in combat (DM tells player if target is above or below ½ HP) Can use 1 Stamina to heal 1D6 HP to self / an ally within melee range, reduce the healing time of a fracture when using a brace by 1D4 intervals, cure hemorrhages with a tailor’s kit, redo a coin flip once made when using unclean items to heal hemorrhages, and can make all the party’s unclean bandages clean as a camp action*  
- **Pilferer:** (FNS \[3\] ACC \[2\]) *Can steal small objects from NPCs (FNS vs.Target’s SRV contest for success; Being hidden, invisible or undetected grants advantage on roll) Many enemies also have rare items that can only be obtained by pilfering (will be indicated on enemy list).*  
- **Performer:** (CHA \[4\] ACC \[2\]) *Can play musical instrument (choose up to 3), sing, or act out scenes in order to impress, entertain or distract others (requires a CHA check)*  
- **Acrobat:** (FNS \[3\] STR \[2\]) *Can perform feats of agility such as somersaults, slides, balancing, dives, etc. without a required roll. Base Jump distance increased to 4+STR / Elevation \+2*  
- **Enchanter:** (INT \[4\]) *Can permanently add enchantments to weapons. Casting advanced enchantments can only be accomplished by the Enchanter class.*  
- **Botanist:** (SRV \[2\] MEM \[2\]) *Gain advantage Nature checks made towards plantlife / fungus; Increase the Ingredient Level of all chemistry items by 1 star in your inventory.*  
- **Tunneler:** (STR \[2\] VIT \[3\]) *Can identify mine spots without a check; Can find places to tunnel to unlock secret paths or treasures.*  
- **Guerilla:** (SPD \[5\]) *Can not be targeted by attacks of opportunity; DMG dealt during ambush attacks deal the weapon's maximum possible value.*  
- **Brawler :** (STR \[2\] SPD \[2\]) *Gain advantage on Martial Maneuvers when unarmed. Fist weapons and unarmed DMG is increased by 1 DT.*  
- **Engineer :** (INT \[4\] ACC \[4\]) *Can craft, repair and modify Autobows, Mortars and Energy Weapons in safe areas like villages and at camp if the player has Forge Hammer and the required material(s); Also a required talent if a player desires to install their own weapons or furnishings on a ship.*

# STAMINA / ABILITIES

The player will start out with a certain amount of “Stamina” points depending on their choices upon character creation. These points deplete after battles and when abilities are used. Stamina can be recovered by eating a meal or resting (see their Recovery & Camping section below) and can be increased by allocating points into the Vitality (VIT) attribute.

**Abilities :**  Special actions a player can perform that are specific to their chosen Tribe, Class, Specialization or can sometimes be learned during gameplay.

**Negative Stamina :** *After losing 1 more pt. Of Stamina while at 0 you become exhausted: Your character loses all Attribute bonuses until they are back within a positive Stamina value. If they are brought down to 0 HP while exhausted they are dead with no VIT save made.*

# MAGIC / SPELLCASTING

**Magic \-** There are seven schools of magic that can be learned depending on a character’s Class, Tribe, etc. The schools of magic are as follows:

-  **Pyromancy**: *The magical art of creating and wielding fire; Highly offensive and sometimes dangerous to even the caster.*  
- **Ocean’s Call Arcana**: *Spells from the ocean depths of water and ice; A well-rounded collection of offensive, support and environment-altering arcana.*  
- **Great Storm Arts**: *A school dedicated to harnessing lightning and the winds; focused on increasing mobility and dealing high damage.*  
- **Twin Moon Arts**: *The arcana granted by the two moons that hang over this world; Magic of light and dark, restorative and destructive, defensive and offensive.*  
-  **Arcadian Arts**: *The magical art of manipulating and summoning plants, earth and creatures alike; Strange spells that poison, entangle, swarm with insects, and more.*  
-  **Phantasm Arts**: *Spells crafted to confound and psych out opponents; Illusions of ear-splitting sound, poisonous mist and invisibility are at your disposal.*


**Spellcasting \-** Successfully casting spells requires the caster to succeed on their Spell Die roll. A player’s Spellcasting Die is determined by their chosen Tribe and the number they must meet or exceed when rolling to determine said spell’s success will be listed beside every spell’s description in the “Magic” document. If a spell’s requirement isn’t met on the first roll the caster can add another spellcasting roll to their attempt on their next turn, adding to a total until the threshold is met. The character’s Intelligence (INT) attribute is added to each spellcasting roll. A successful spell activates its described effect(s) upon a successful Spellcasting roll, no target evasion, accuracy or any other type of roll is typically needed. 

***\*Any spells cast outside of combat cost the caster STAMINA: 1 pt. per Spell Lv.** (Novice \= 1 Cost / Advanced \= 2 Cost / Master \= 3 Cost)*

**Learning Spells \-** The amount of spells a character can learn at once is determined by their Spell Memory stat, which is specified by their chosen Tribe and increased by their Memory (MEM) attribute value. The character’s current learned spells can be reshuffled each time they level up or by using a Seeker Stone (a rare consumable) while at camp. Different schools of magic, each with their own unique spells, can be learned by Leveling up depending on your chosen Class and / or Tribe. Spells conferred while wearing magic rings or other equipment items do not require a slot to learn.

**Core Spells \-** Spells that are not affiliated with any of the six schools of magic and are learned from Advancement Bonuses, Specializations, or Starting Bonuses are considered ‘Core Spells’, meaning they are always memorized and they do not take up any Spell Memory slots.

# LUCK TOKENS

Luck Tokens are points a player can earn by participating in the game through various means that can be used to turn any 1D20 roll into a coin flip (heads \= success / tails \= failure). This can be called at any point, even directly after a failed 1D20 roll.

- **Any First-Try Success on a roll made utilizing a known Talent**  
- **One-Round Victory in Combat**  
- **Critical 20 Rolls in Combat or on Attribute Checks**

***Each player can only have 2 Luck Tokens banked at a time; Some classes / tribes may be able to increase this total by choosing certain level-up bonuses.***

# TRAVERSING THE OVERWORLD

The overworld map will be represented by a grid, much like a battle map would be. The party chooses which of their players is in the lead of the group as they traverse the map. The party then decides their method of travel:

***The Three S’s of Overworld Travel:***

- **SWIFTLY** (SPD) *traverse the overworld as quickly as possible. Leader makes a 1D6+SPD roll for overworld tiles moved*  
- **SURVEYING** (SRV) *comb over the area looking for hidden areas, ruins, materials, etc. Leader makes a 1D4 roll for overworld tiles moved and a 1D20+SRV roll to determine discoveries.*  
- **STEALTH** (FNS) *traverse the overworld slowly so as to not draw attention to the group. Leader makes a 1D4 roll for overworld tiles moved. The chance of a Random Encounter is lowered by the leader’s FNS.*


***All forms of travel have a chance to incur a Random Encounter. These will be determined by the Module being used during your current game***

A sinister black substance that seeps into this world from the Deathplane, Mhirid that kills living beings on contact. It began appearing after Mhire gained control over Mhirid once the storm of corrupted souls known as the Deathblight formed there as a result of the thousands of deaths wrought by the Ruination. The party may encounter this extremely dangerous substance deep within dungeons and caverns where the God of Corrupted Dead’s influence can be felt.

**The Fey**  
Strange, mischievous creatures from a parallel plane sometimes appear in Hidra Allath; twisted, mirrored versions of our realms’ plants, animals and even monsters that disrupt the relative order of the world. They are known as Fey, Fairy, or Sylvan creatures and they exude an otherworldly aura that sometimes defies logic or nature. It is not known why they appear before the people of Hidra Allath, or if their desires can even be understood by mortals. What can be said for certain is that their reality-bending aura can be disrupted by pure silver and that their presence can often be felt before they have been seen. They appear where the barriers between our reality and the other dimensions are weakest.

**Naval Ranks**  
Upon defeating an enemy ship in naval combat the party will gain 1 “Commendation” (basically a Merit Point exclusively denoting naval combat mastery). Upon reaching certain thresholds of Commendations the party gains a Rank, granting them new abilities, bonuses and actions while sailing ships.

* **Rank 1: Seagull** *(2 Commendation) \-* Regain 1D10 ship HP upon reaching 0 (once per battle)  
* **Rank 2: Tern** *(6 Commendation) \-* Gain the ability to own a second ship (captained by another player or a hired crew member)  
* **Rank 3: Pelican** *(12 Commendation) \-* Advanced Ships can now be owned by the party  
* **Rank 4: Cormorant** *(20 Commendation) \-* Crew no longer needs to be paid (crew pays themselves with loot- this does not effect items the party can obtain)  
* **Rank 5: Petrel** *(30 Commendation) \-* Gain a 3rd ship action during naval combat  
* **Rank 6: Albatross** *(50 Commendation) \-* All ship weapons gain an additional 1D6 to HIT and DMG rolls

# RECOVERY / CAMPING

- **FULL RECOVERY** : The party is fully healed, stamina is restored, magic items recharged, status effects are ended and timers / cooldowns on effects are zeroed out. This happens when the party stays at an inn or other place of rest in a safe area of the world or at specified times during campaign / side-quest progress.  
- **ITEMS :** Each player can use as many consumable items as they want outside of combat  
- **HEALING SPELLS :** Spells cast outside of combat, including HP / status effect recovery spells (takes 1 Stamina pt. to cast outside of combat).  
- **RESTING :** A rest can be taken at any point outside of combat. This restores HD6+SRV HP and STAM to each player. The party has 1 rest they can use but this total resets after a camping phase / full recovery  
- **CAMPING :** The party can choose to set up camp until dawn breaks the next morning which fully restores HP, restores the charge of magic items and eliminates status effects. Players can also use as many items as they wish from their inventory. Each player gets 1 camping action they can perform as well:


**\*If a player ends a camping phase without eating they suffer \-1 Stamina** *(except for Tenant players)*

- **Keep Watch :** The player acts as the camp’s sentry keeping an eye out for hostile forces and enemy ambushes (1D20+SRV check)   
  - **Roast :** The player makes a simple meal using only 1 food item (no talent required, but only 1 possible benefit can be gained this way)  
  - **Change Spells :** (Seeker Stone required) The player reconfigures which spells they currently have memorized  
  - **Focus Aura (*Tenant only*) \-** Recover 1D6+MEM Stamina pts. Through meditation which helps the Tenant’s physical form stay constituted  
  - **Sanitize Campsite :** The player cleans the campsite making sure bedrolls, cooking equipment and medical tools are washed and cleared of filth (1D20+SRV check ***\[8+ \= success / 7- \= Party members may suffer disease; each player makes a VIT check if this occur \[8+ \= they remain disease-free, 7- \= DM rolls them a random disease***\] )  
  - **Reconnaissance :** The player chooses to scout out the surrounding area; Make a “Survey Area” Traversal roll which may reveal anything hidden in a 3x3 tile area surrounding the overworld tile the party is camping on.

# ENCUMBRANCE / INVENTORY

Each player has an Encumbrance stat noted on their Tribe sheet (*Starting value \+ STR*). This value governs how much gear each player can carry on their person. Each weapon, piece of armor, tool and offhand item will usually weigh enough to contribute to the player’s total encumbrance while small items like potions and keys will not.

**Pack Beetles:**  
Upon reaching Inuva the first pioneers that made landfall were occasionally approached by large, non-hostile insects that were soon put to work by the explorers as beasts of burden. They were round, about four feet in diameter and crawled flat on the ground resembling massive lady beetles. The creatures were named Pack Beetles, as they had evolved large cavities under their shells that they used to store food and other supplies and were evidently used by the original rulers of Inuva to do the same as the newcomers had learned to do.

- If the players have a Pack Beetle in the party they can store up to **10 Weight** worth of items with them.  
- Accessing their Pack Beetles’ inventory requires the party to rest, make camp, or be in town / a safe zone.  
- Their Pack Beetle will follow them around everywhere possible, they can even fly a very short distance but they only ever do so in order to follow the party, they can not do this to scout ahead or retrieve distant items, etc.  
- Pack Beetles come in every color and with an infinite number of shell patterns. The party can also name theirs if they don’t already have a name.

# STEALTH

At any time a player can begin sneaking, attempting to make as little noise as possible and perform their actions without drawing attention to themselves. They player in question rolls 1D20+FNS while NPC’s / monsters within their area roll 1D20+SRV (related groups of enemies make 1 check collectively) Once it has been determined that the player has successfully began sneaking they can perform quiet actions (listed below) or gain advantage on their next action that breaks stealth like attacking an enemy. 

Equipped armor may inhibit Stealth based on its heaviness (Gloves and Helmets do not affect stealth unless specifically stated)

- Light Armor / Clothing: does not inhibit stealth  
- Medium Armor: FNS bonus is not added to Stealth rolls  
- Heavy Armor: Stealth rolls are made with disadvantage

**(Example of actions that can be taken during stealth)**

- Lock Picking  
- Trap Disarming  
- Picking up small items

(**Examples of actions that end stealth)**

- Attacking a target (*unless the target is defeated and no other enemies see this*)  
- Speaking aloud to another NPC / character  
- Opening / closing doors, chests, crates, etc.


# RANDOM ENCOUNTERS

While traversing the overworld the party may incur a random encounter. The nature of these encounters will be determined by the setting of the current game but they will all adhere to this general structure:

- The DM rolls 1D20 to determine if enemies are in the path chosen by the players  
  - **CRITICAL SUCCESS (20) :** Party can move an additional 1D4 tiles  
  - **20 \- 5 :** The path is uneventful  
  - **4 \- 1 :** Random Encounter occurs  
  - **CRITICAL FAILURE (1) :** Random Encounter occurs as an ambush  
- Using the traversal method “Move Swiftly” increases the likelihood of an unforeseen random encounter by 1D4 while “Move Covertly” lowers the likelihood by the value of the leader’s FNS attribute.

# MODIFYING / ENCHANTING

Some equipment may have additional effects / uses due to being modified with atypical materials or enchanted with a permanent spell. Modified and Enchanted gear can be found in dungeons, on defeated enemies as loot, or created by players.

**Modifying:** A character with the Armorer Talent can change the properties of a common piece of gear in town or at camp using a Forge Hammer and what is known as an “Eye Stone” (listed in the ‘Items’ document- ‘Mods & Enchanting’ section).  
**Enchanting:** A character with the Enchanting Talent can use a rare item called a Chromatic Bead and cast a permanent magical buff on a piece of gear. The enchanter only has access to certain enchantments based on their enchanting level (see Talent section for table) and based on the schools of magic they have access to (e.g. only a pyromancer can grant the ‘Fire’ enchantment to a weapon).

**Only 1 Modification and 1 Enchantment can be granted to a piece of gear- said gear must be ‘Common’ with no Sub-Type (Bronze, Starmetal, etc.) These additions are permanent once selected; Mod Materials or Chromatic Beads can not be retrieved once used.**

# ACQUIRING MATERIALS

While traversing the overworld or a dungeon party members can decide that they wish to search for materials by mining ore deposits, gathering local flora, or scavenging ruins for scrap.

**Mining \-** *(STR check) when an ore deposit is found a pickaxe (tool) can be used on it to gain ore and metals (decided by area the ore deposit exists within)*  
**Digging \-** *(STR check) using a shovel (tool) dig through the ground to find suspected buried items of interest*  
**Foraging \-** *(SRV check) search around to find usable flora and fauna, typically in the form of chemistry ingredients but occasionally crafting material will be found (dependant on area)*  
**Scavenging \-** *(FNS check) pick through an old ruin or collapsed structure for usable materials, forsaken equipment, etc. (dependent on the area and nature of the ruined structure).*

# CURRENCY / COMMERCE

**Silver Coins**  
In the current age of this world the major traders and services use silver coins (SV) as the main agreed-upon currency. Historically gold, while valuable itself,  is primarily used in magical and alchemical experiments due to its strange properties of acting as a conduit / retaining the effects of arcane energies. These coins have been given many colloquial names throughout the years: “Shiners”, “Silver Leaves”, and “Jacktears” to name a few.  
**Marks of Parha**  
Ancient platinum coins first minted by the Azdan Gnomes, easily identified by their brilliant shine and a stamp of a snake on its face. In the ancient world, the Azdan Gnomes had the most profitable factions of merchants to do business with regardless of your own affiliation, therefore their currency was extremely valuable across High Thaul. Even today these marks are accepted in lieu of silver in many types of transactions, especially those that pertain to artifacts of the Arthosian Age.  
**Bartering & trading**  
In this era of exploration and the reestablishment of civilization most if not all merchants will also be willing to barter for goods or for services provided by the player characters. Characters with a high Charisma (CHA) attribute will have an advantage during trade negotiations.

# GLOSSARY

**SV \-** Short for Silver, the standard coin / currency of this part of the world  
**ATK \-** (Attack) Roll made to see if an attack is successful  
**DMG \-** (Damage) Roll made to determine how much HP a target loses in an attack  
**DT \-** (Die Tier) Usually in terms of raising or lowering the die used to make a non-1D20 roll. The order is as follows: 1D4, 1D6, 1D8, 1D10, 1D12. If a 1D4 roll is lowered a DT it becomes a LDR instead, as 1D4 is the smallest value die used in this game.  
**Advantage \-** when in effect two 1D20 rolls are made and the higher is accepted  
**Disadvantage \-** when in effect two 1D20 rolls are made and the lower is accepted  
**HD\# \-** (High Die Roll) when in effect roll the specified die twice and the higher roll is accepted  
**LD\# \-** (Low Die Roll) when in effect roll the specified die twice and the higher roll is accepted  
**Dur. \-** Short for “Durability”, the amount an item can be used before breaking / becoming unusable  
**Rnd. / Rnds. \-** Foreshortening of “Round(s)”, the amount of in-combat rounds an effect lasts.  
**Rng** \- Foreshortening of “Range”, as in the amount of tiles an ATK or spell can be used within  
**MAT \-** Short for “Crafting Material”, in reference to materials used to craft new items  
**ING \-** Short for “Chemistry Ingredient”, in reference to items used to make potions, poisons, etc.  
**MOD \-** Short for “Mod Material”, in reference to items used to modify existing weapons / armor.  
**Misc \-** Short for “Miscellaneous”, in the context of this game meaning any item/object that has no mechanical use, description, stats, designation, etc. (such as the Wooden Flute that is dropped by the Grummelbok enemies- it is used by the creature but has no inherent value to the player, yet they may wish to obtain it for role-playing purposes).
